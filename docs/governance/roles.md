# Platform Roles & Governance

## User Roles

### Seeker (Free)
- Browse public library
- Read public texts and articles
- Join public discussions
- View public journals

### Member ($9/month)
- All Seeker features
- Personal journal and annotations
- Reading progress tracking
- Personal collections
- AI Study Assistant access
- Member-only discussions

### Scholar ($25/month)
- All Member features
- Scholar verification badge
- Private study circles
- Advanced AI tools
- Rare archive access
- Early access to new features

### Curator
- Appointed role (not purchased)
- Review and classify submitted content
- Approve/reject text additions
- Manage taxonomy and glossary

### Moderator
- Appointed role
- Moderate discussions and comments
- Handle reports
- Enforce community standards

### Admin
- Full platform access
- Manage all content and users
- Analytics access
- Configuration and settings

## Content Governance

### Curator Council
Eventually, The Living Library will establish a Curator Council of:
- Academic scholars in relevant fields
- Specialists in specific traditions
- Librarians and archivists
- Digital humanities experts

The Council will:
- Review canon classifications
- Arbitrate contested content
- Develop curriculum for learning paths
- Ensure civilisational respect policies

### Anti-Concentration of Power

No single person or perspective should dominate the platform. We implement:
- Multiple curator roles across traditions
- Transparent decision-making processes
- Public editorial policies
- Mechanisms for challenging classifications

## Community Standards

### What We Encourage
- Long-form, citation-backed discussion
- Respectful engagement with all traditions
- Scholarly humility about uncertain claims
- Cross-tradition dialogue with nuance

### What Is Not Permitted
- Personality worship or guru dynamics
- Political extremism dressed as spirituality
- Conspiracy theories and pseudo-history
- Harassment or disrespect toward traditions
- Spam, low-quality, or off-topic content

## Civilisational Respect Policy

The platform is committed to:
- Preserving cultural context and historical specificity
- Not forcing equivalencies between different traditions
- Not appropriating sacred systems for entertainment
- Representing traditions on their own terms
- Including voices from within traditions, not just external observers

Indigenous traditions in particular require:
- Community consultation before representation
- Acknowledgment of oral vs. written knowledge
- Respect for sacred/secret knowledge boundaries
- Credit to specific communities, not generic "indigenous wisdom"
